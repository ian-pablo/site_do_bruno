His Titles at Barcelona

34 Titles:

10 La Ligas, 7 Copa do Rei, 8 Supercopa de España

4 UEFA Champions League, 3 Supercopa da UEFA, 3 FIFA Club World Cup

Highlight Seasons:

2008–09: The First Treble Titles: La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League, Supercopa de España Highlight: Under Pep Guardiola’s leadership, Messi played a central role in Barcelona’s first-ever treble. He scored in all competitions and was crucial in the Champions League final victory over Manchester United, where he scored a memorable header. The team was renowned for its captivating football, dominating both Spain and Europe.

2009–10: The Year of the Sextuple Titles: La Liga, Supercopa de España, UEFA Super Cup, FIFA Club World Cup (plus the previous season’s treble) Highlight: Barcelona became the first team in history to win a sextuple, securing every possible title in a year. Messi scored the decisive goal in the Club World Cup final against Estudiantes with a chest finish, cementing his status as the world’s best player. This historic feat marked the peak of Barcelona’s dominance.

2010–11: The Brilliant Champions League Season Titles: La Liga, UEFA Champions League, Supercopa de España Highlight: This season is often considered one of Barcelona’s best. The team triumphed 3-1 against Manchester United in the Champions League final at Wembley, in a performance widely regarded as one of the greatest ever. Messi scored a vital goal and was named Man of the Match, reinforcing his global supremacy.

2014–15: The Second Treble Titles: La Liga, Copa del Rey, UEFA Champions League Highlight: Under Luis Enrique, Messi, alongside Neymar and Luis Suárez, formed the MSN attacking trio, scoring over 120 goals that season. Messi’s performance against Bayern Munich in the Champions League semi-final, where he dribbled past Jérôme Boateng and chipped Manuel Neuer, is remembered as one of his most iconic moments. Barcelona defeated Juventus 3-1 in the final to secure their second treble.

2020–21: Last Title with Barça Titles: Copa del Rey Highlight: This season was significant as it marked Messi’s final trophy with Barcelona. In the Copa del Rey final against Athletic Bilbao, Messi shone with two goals and led the team to a 4-0 victory. This title symbolized his dedication and talent, even during challenging times for the club.
